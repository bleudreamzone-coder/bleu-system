our first state

Louisiana ranks last in healthcare outcomes. That is where we start.

The platform is built for every state. Deployment in Louisiana comes first because the need is the largest. 73% of Louisianans live in a primary-care shortage area. 93% in a mental-health shortage area. 44 of 64 parishes are designated rural. Louisiana ranks second nationally in rural hospitals at risk of closure.

who shows up here

Built for the people most often lost to escalation.

The platform is free at the point of use, statewide by design, and built specifically for the populations the state already pays for.

i.

Rural residents across Louisiana's 60+ rural parishes — especially in primary-care shortage areas and parishes whose hospital has closed, scaled back, or sits on the at-risk list.

ii.

Medicaid members and dual-eligibles whose access is shaped by transportation, distance, and provider availability.

iii.

The uninsured and underinsured — including the working poor disqualified from Medicaid but unable to absorb commercial costs.

iv.

FQHC, RHC, and parish health unit patients whose between-visit guidance is currently unavailable.

v.

Pregnant and postpartum patients facing the OB desert, with hypertensive disorders as a primary contributor to Louisiana's elevated maternal mortality.

vi.

Adults in addiction recovery who need non-judgmental, off-hours support and routing to peer recovery, SAMHSA, and 988.

vii.

Older adults managing multiple chronic conditions, polypharmacy, and supplement-medication interaction risk.

viii.

Veterans and rural caregivers carrying chronic disease, behavioral health burden, and isolation.
