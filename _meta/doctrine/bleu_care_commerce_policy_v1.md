# BLEU Care-Routing & Commerce Integrity Policy — v1

**Authority:** Dr. Felicia Stoler, DCN, MS, RDN, FACSM, FAND, Dipl. ACLM — President & Chief Clinical/Medical/Wellness Officer, bleu.live
**Last reviewed:** 2026-05-31
**Status:** Canonical doctrine. Internal — not for external distribution.
**Grounds:** Walker, Muench, Perlis & Vargas (2022), *Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia (CBT-I): A Primer*, *Klin Spec Psihol* 11(2):123–137; American College of Physicians first-line recommendation for chronic insomnia (Qaseem et al., 2016).

---

## 1. Operating assumption
BLEU is the **first line** for people who often cannot easily reach the healthcare system — no insurance, rural or resource-poor areas, no available appointment, or prior negative experiences with providers. We assume the person may have **no extra money to spend**. Our first job is to give them what they can do for themselves, grounded in lifestyle-medicine principles: nutrition, physical activity, sleep, stress management, avoidance of risky substances, and social connection / purpose.

## 2. The four routing pillars
- **Self** — what the person can do themselves (free behavioral/lifestyle guidance; the *only* pillar where optional commerce ever appears).
- **Community** — peer / community support. Always offered. Never a sale.
- **Clinician** — *human clinical care*: doctors, FQHCs, clinics, hospitals, rehab, behavioral health. A referral, not a sale.
- **Crisis** — crisis and emergency routing. Hard-stops all commerce.

**Critical definition:** *Supplements and products are NOT clinical.* "Clinical" means the human care above. A supplement is one optional thing a person may choose for themselves; it lives only in **Self**, and it comes **last**.

## 3. The universal response ladder (every concern)
For any non-crisis concern, a response is ordered:
1. **Lead with free, evidence-based behavioral / lifestyle guidance** specific to the concern.
2. **Always offer Community and/or Clinician support** (human; free or referral; never monetized).
3. **Only after step 1, and only as an explicit option / by consent,** may a supplement or comfort good appear — clearly optional, clearly non-clinical, placed last.
4. **Crisis short-circuits everything:** crisis + care routing only; zero commerce.

**Implementation note (important):** The rule is **ordering inside the answer**, not turn-position. A supplement may appear in a first response *only if* the free guidance precedes it in that same response and it is framed as an option. "No commerce on turn one" is a crude proxy; the real gate is *free guidance first, optional item last.*

## 4. Commerce taxonomy

| Category | Monetize? | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| **Supplements** (e.g., magnesium, L-theanine) | Yes | Optional, last, by consent; honest evidence framing; never presented as treatment. |
| **Comfort / environment goods** (sleep mask, weighted blanket, cooling sheets/fan, coloring set + pencils, paper journal) | Yes | Optional, last; map to legitimate self-care / sleep environment. |
| **The clinical recommendation itself** (the evidence-based treatment, the free apps, the act of routing to Community / Clinician / Crisis) | **Never** | We recommend what works even when it earns nothing. This is the line between "clinically governed" and "a store with a chatbot." |
| **Measurement / wearable devices** (sleep trackers, BP monitors, BIA / body scales) | Handle with care | Never pushed in a concern they don't belong to. **Sleep trackers are excluded from the insomnia flow** (risk of *orthosomnia* — tracking worsens anxious sleepers). Ask whether the person **already owns** a device rather than prompting a purchase. Clinical metrics (e.g., blood pressure) route to a clinician with "share your readings with your provider" — never sold as self-management. |
| **Treatment & service referrals** (BetterHelp, rehab / SUD facilities, clinics, clinical programs) | **Legal review required** | Intended revenue stream, but **do not build paid routing until cleared by counsel.** Rehab / SUD referral fees: **do-not-build** pending review (EKRA / anti-kickback / state patient-brokering exposure). Any affiliate relationship requires clear FTC disclosure. |

## 5. Evidence-tier discipline
Every recommendation carries its evidence weight on the BLEU evidence ladder (**Established / Emerging / Experimental / Narrative**). Never present an *Emerging* item as *Established*. The monetizable item is never the headline.
- *Established:* CBT-I for chronic insomnia (ACP first-line).
- *Emerging:* magnesium, L-theanine; expressive journaling and art/coloring for anxiety.
- The free, evidence-based intervention always outranks and precedes the paid option.

## 6. Safety guardrails
- For mental-health concerns (anxiety, depression), a **crisis screen precedes** any self-help framing. Never let a comfort-good suggestion minimize serious symptoms.
- **Clinician off-ramp** for severe, long-standing, or comorbid presentations.
- **Sleep restriction** carries a caution: it intentionally builds short-term daytime sleepiness ("go easy if you drive") and warrants supervision for certain conditions.

## 7. Worked examples

### 7a. Sleep / insomnia
1. **Free & evidence-based first.** CBT-I is first-line (*Established*). Core = **sleep restriction + stimulus control**; sleep hygiene is adjunctive (weak alone). The engine is a **sleep diary**.
   - Free self-help tools: **Insomnia Coach** (VA) is the flagship for users *without* a provider; **CBT-i Coach** (VA) is the diary/companion if/when they connect with a clinician; reputable free education (e.g., Sleep Foundation).
2. **Always offer Community / Clinician** (a CBT-I specialist for severe or comorbid cases).
3. **Optional, last, by consent:** magnesium / L-theanine (*Emerging*); sleep mask, weighted blanket, cooling (comfort goods).
4. **Excluded from sale here:** sleep trackers (orthosomnia). Ask only about devices already owned, and even then steer back to the diary.
- *Caution:* sleep-restriction drowsiness.

### 7b. Anxiety / depression
1. **Free & evidence-based first:** lifestyle pillars (movement, sleep, connection, stress reduction), breathing / relaxation, free self-help.
2. **Always offer Community AND Clinician; mandatory crisis screen.**
3. **Optional, last, by consent:** coloring / mandala set + pencils, paper journal (comfort / *Emerging*). Never lead with these — the comfort good is an *addition* to support, never a stand-in.

## 8. The litmus test
Before any monetizable item appears, ask: **"Would we still recommend this if it earned us nothing?"** If the paid item is leading the response, stop and re-order. If a recommendation is a *treatment or service* with a fee attached, it goes to legal review first.
