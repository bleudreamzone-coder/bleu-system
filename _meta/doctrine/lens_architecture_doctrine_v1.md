## Raw AI is light. BLEU is the lens.

A general model is a floodlight: maximal brightness, no focus, no protection. Pointed at a vulnerable person it illuminates everything indiscriminately, including the dangerous answer. A lens does not generate light — it **focuses, filters, and protects**. BLEU sits between the floodlight (the general model) and the person, turning raw output into a safe, focused, lowest-regret next step.

---

## The five machines

The lens is built from five machines in series. Output of each is the input of the next. (This is the engineering form of the seven gates in [[coca_cola_recipe_v1]].)

1. **Signal** — read the moment. Detect the real need beneath the words (sleep, stress, gut, energy, mood, pain, crisis, general) and the user's state.
2. **Safety** — screen for harm. Crisis floor (988), interactions, contraindications, red flags. This machine can halt all the others.
3. **Route** — choose the lowest-regret action (calm / learn / protocol / product / practitioner / track / escalate) via the LRAS.
4. **Outcome** — schedule and capture the follow-up (day-3 / 7 / 30). A route with no outcome check is not finished.
5. **Memory** — record what happened so the next Signal→Route is sharper. This is where the compounding moat lives.

> Signal → Safety → Route → Outcome → Memory. Break the chain and you have a chatbot. Keep the chain and you have a lens that learns.

## The three voices

Every machine speaks in all three (see [[source_document_v1]]): **user** (warmth), **clinical** (restraint), **infrastructure** (durability). A machine missing a voice is a defect.

## Seven moves to irreplaceability

1. **Govern the decision**, not just the answer (the gates).
2. **Measure outcomes**, not engagement (the day-N loop).
3. **Compound the memory** — every outcome sharpens the next route.
4. **Name a clinical reviewer** and make review a gate (Felicia DCN).
5. **Refuse visibly** — restraint competitors can't afford to copy.
6. **Hash and audit everything** — trust as infrastructure (TD-010, RLS, idempotency).
7. **Route toward humans** — community and practitioners, never a walled garden.

Each move costs short-term metrics, which is precisely why incumbents won't copy them — and why they become a moat.

## The 28-year operator principle

The founder operated in service and hospitality for ~28 years before building BLEU. The principle carried over: **you earn the right to recommend by first making the person feel safe and seen.** A great operator reads the room, protects the guest, and never upsells someone in distress. The lens encodes that instinct into software — feel → validate → solve, and never sell during crisis.
