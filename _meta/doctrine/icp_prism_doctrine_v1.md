## DOCTRINE PURPOSE

The flat ICP framework treats each customer category as a single profile. That framework is correct at the strategic level — five buyer doors, priority order, severance from CannaIQ — but it is **insufficient at the operational level.**

The operational reality: **a 38-year-old perimenopausal woman with anxiety and a Lexapro prescription is not the same Citizen as a 62-year-old man with Type 2 diabetes and supplement curiosity.** Both fit "ICP 1: Self-Paying Wellness Seeker." Neither should receive the same BLEU experience.

**This doctrine introduces ICP variant classification using a Prism-style band system.** Each Citizen is classified across six bands. The intersection of bands defines the variant. The variant determines:

- Onboarding question sequence
- State classification calibration
- Dose tolerance ceiling
- Voice and tone shaping
- Commerce timing rules
- Clinical escalation thresholds
- Routing decisions
- Outcome metric weights

This is the bridge between BLEU's category positioning ("Adaptive Behavioral Operating System") and BLEU's actual operational delivery. **Without variant awareness, BLEU treats every Citizen the same. With variant awareness, BLEU lives up to its category claim.**

# PART 1 — THE SIX BANDS

Like Prism separates a signal into bands (intent, urgency, state, etc.), ICP variants are classified across six independent bands. Each band has 3-5 discrete values. The full variant space is the Cartesian product, but most combinations are rare or impossible. Common combinations form named variants (Part 3-7).

## Band 1 — Life Stage

The chronological + biological + life-circumstance position of the Citizen.

| Value | Description | Common needs |
|---|---|---|
| **L1: Emerging Adult** (18-29) | Building habits, first independence, identity formation | Sleep, stress, body composition, anxiety, identity coherence |
| **L2: Establishing Adult** (30-44) | Career building, partnering, possibly parenting | Stress, energy, sleep debt, weight management, fertility/pregnancy adjacent, early metabolic concerns |
| **L3: Midlife Pivot** (45-59) | Peak responsibility, perimenopause/andropause, chronic disease emergence | Sleep architecture changes, hormonal shifts, metabolic syndrome onset, longevity awareness, chronic pain, mental health |
| **L4: Active Aging** (60-74) | Retirement transition, healthspan focus, identity reformation | Cognitive longevity, mobility preservation, polypharmacy management, social connection, purpose |
| **L5: Late Aging** (75+) | Compression of morbidity focus, family-mediated decisions | Frailty prevention, polypharmacy critical, caregiver coordination, dignity preservation |

**Note on edges:** L1-L2 boundary at 30 is fuzzy. L3-L4 at 60 is fuzzy. Cultural and biological factors shift these by 5-10 years. BLEU should not be rigidly age-gated — it should accept Citizen self-description with light age verification.

## Band 2 — Clinical Complexity

The presence and complexity of clinical conditions, medications, and care relationships. **THIS BAND REQUIRES DR. FELICIA CLINICAL SIGNOFF for boundary placement.**

| Value | Description | BLEU posture |
|---|---|---|
| **C0: Asymptomatic Wellness Curious** | No diagnoses, no medications, general wellness interest | Full general guidance, broad protocol library, full commerce permission |
| **C1: Functional Concerns** | Symptoms without diagnoses (sleep issues, low energy, mild anxiety, brain fog) | Symptom-targeted protocols, lifestyle medicine emphasis, supplement guidance with general safety |
| **C2: Single Stable Condition** | One diagnosed condition (mild hypertension, Type 2 diabetes well-controlled, mild depression on stable med) | Condition-aware protocols, drug-supplement interaction screening, clinician-care coordination encouraged |
| **C3: Multiple/Complex Conditions** | Multiple diagnoses, polypharmacy (3+ meds), recent hospitalizations, autoimmune, cancer history | Heightened safety screening, clinician-care PRIMARY (BLEU augments not replaces), conservative supplement guidance, mandatory escalation protocols |
| **C4: Acute/Unstable** | Recently diagnosed serious condition, mental health crisis, post-procedure recovery, active substance use | Crisis-aware posture, commerce-OFF, clinician-routing PRIMARY, BLEU as supportive presence not advisor |

**Critical doctrine:** BLEU's response calibration must be MOST CONSERVATIVE at C3-C4. The default if uncertain about clinical complexity is to assume HIGHER complexity until information surfaces otherwise. False positives (treating C2 as C3) are acceptable. False negatives (treating C3 as C1) are dangerous.

## Band 3 — Financial Capacity

The Citizen's ability and willingness to pay for wellness products, services, and BLEU itself. **Codex implementation note:** This band must NEVER be used to limit clinical safety information. Financial capacity affects PRODUCT routing, not SAFETY routing.

| Value | Description | Commerce posture |
|---|---|---|
| **F1: Constrained** | Cost is primary decision factor; may be on Medicaid, SNAP, unemployment, fixed income | Free guidance prioritized, low-cost OTC alternatives, no premium-only paths, community resource routing emphasized |
| **F2: Practical** | Will pay for clear value, comparison shops, cautious | Mid-tier products emphasized, Amazon affiliate appropriate, subscription clearly justified by value |
| **F3: Comfortable** | Disposable income for wellness, will pay for quality | Full product library, Fullscript practitioner-dispensed appropriate, subscription tier appropriate |
| **F4: Premium** | High disposable income, expects concierge experience, longevity-investment posture | Premium tier appropriate, advanced testing protocols, multi-product stacks, personalized practitioner relationships |

**Doctrine rule:** Citizens self-describe financial capacity through behavioral signals (purchase declines, "is there a cheaper option," "what's the best regardless of cost") — BLEU does NOT ask financial capacity directly. It infers from interaction patterns and adjusts routing accordingly.

## Band 4 — Readiness

The Citizen's stage in the behavior-change cycle, mapped roughly to the Transtheoretical Model (Prochaska/DiClemente) but simplified for BLEU's operational use.

| Value | Description | BLEU response posture |
|---|---|---|
| **R1: Exploring** | Curious but not committed, gathering information, low effort tolerance | Education-forward, no plans yet, light touch, "save for later" appropriate |
| **R2: Considering** | Recognizing need, weighing options, not ready to act | Comparison framing, evidence emphasis, gentle nudges, "when you're ready" language |
| **R3: Preparing** | Decided to act, planning logistics, asking specifics | Plan generation, friction reduction, "start with one thing" specificity |
| **R4: Acting** | Implementing changes, tracking progress, needs reinforcement | Adherence support, troubleshooting, micro-wins celebration, plan adjustments |
| **R5: Maintaining** | Sustained change, occasional regression, long-term integration | Drift monitoring, refresh suggestions, expansion opportunities, identity reinforcement |
| **R0: Pre-Contemplation** | Doesn't yet see the need (rare in BLEU since arrival itself signals R1+) | Listen, validate, don't push, leave door open |

**Doctrine rule:** Readiness is the most volatile band. A Citizen can move from R3 to R1 in a single session if they encounter friction. BLEU must re-classify readiness frequently, not assume stability.

## Band 5 — Trust

The Citizen's current level of trust in BLEU specifically. Begins at default (T2) and moves based on interactions.

| Value | Description | BLEU response posture |
|---|---|---|
| **T0: Hostile** | Actively skeptical, testing BLEU, prepared to leave | Earn trust through honesty, minimal claims, "we're not for everyone" language |
| **T1: Cautious** | New to BLEU, wary of AI wellness, vetting | Demonstrate clinical governance, show audit posture, explain "what we won't do" |
| **T2: Default** | Neutral, willing to engage, neither skeptical nor enthusiastic | Standard onboarding, transparent expectations |
| **T3: Engaged** | Returning, completing recommendations, providing feedback | Deeper personalization unlocks, longer plans appropriate, commerce gates more permissive |
| **T4: Advocate** | Sharing BLEU with others, completing long-term protocols, treating BLEU as trusted advisor | Premium experiences appropriate, referral mechanics, beta access offers appropriate |

**Doctrine rule:** Trust can drop multiple levels in a single interaction (a wrong recommendation, a missed crisis signal, a too-soon commerce ask). Trust climbs slowly. BLEU should treat trust as the most valuable currency and protect it like balance sheet capital.

## Band 6 — Dose Tolerance

How much information, complexity, and action the Citizen can absorb in a single interaction without overwhelm. Closely related to the "Friction Budget" concept from the v5.0 infographic.

| Value | Description | Response dose |
|---|---|---|
| **D1: Micro** | Can handle 1 small action, simple language only | 1 step, <50 words, single concept, no choices |
| **D2: Low** | 1-2 steps, brief framing, light reasoning | 2 steps max, <150 words, simple choice (A or B) |
| **D3: Medium** | 3-6 steps, modest framing, evidence summary | Standard protocol delivery, comparison appropriate |
| **D4: High** | 7+ steps, detailed protocols, multi-week plans, nuance welcomed | Full protocol library, comparative analysis, deep dives appropriate |
| **D5: Expert** | Wants citations, methodology, mechanism explanations, scientific depth | Full clinical reasoning visible, evidence ladder explicit, advanced protocol access |

**Doctrine rule:** Dose tolerance is highly state-dependent. The same Citizen can be D4 on a Tuesday morning and D1 on a Friday night after a hard week. BLEU must classify dose tolerance per-session, not as a fixed Citizen attribute.

# PART 2 — THE BAND INTERACTION RULES

Bands do not operate independently. Certain band combinations trigger automatic safety overrides or routing decisions.

## Override Rule 1: Clinical Complexity Dominates

If Band 2 (Clinical Complexity) = C3 or C4, then:
- Band 3 (Financial) does NOT determine safety information access — all safety-critical info is free regardless
- Band 4 (Readiness) does NOT accelerate commerce — even R4 Citizens get conservative commerce gates
- Band 6 (Dose) defaults DOWN by one level — overwhelmed C3 Citizens get D1 dosing minimum

## Override Rule 2: Acute Trust Loss

If Band 5 (Trust) drops from T2+ to T0/T1 within a single session:
- Commerce gates close immediately
- Routing defaults to "less is more" mode
- Apology + acknowledgment protocol fires
- Next session begins with extra grace

## Override Rule 3: Crisis Trumps All

If any signal in the conversation indicates active crisis (suicidal ideation, self-harm, acute substance use, medical emergency):
- All bands suspend
- Crisis routing activates
- Commerce permanently off for the session
- Human handoff bridge invoked

## Override Rule 4: Dose Floor on Vulnerability

If Band 1 = L5 (Late Aging) OR Band 2 = C3-C4 OR Band 5 = T0:
- Maximum dose = D2 regardless of other bands
- This is the "vulnerability floor" — never overwhelm a vulnerable Citizen

## Override Rule 5: Adolescent Boundary (forward-looking)

BLEU does NOT serve minors (under 18). If any signal suggests Citizen is under 18:
- Immediate adult-redirect protocol
- No commerce
- No clinical guidance
- Suggest age-appropriate resources

This is a hard boundary, not a band classification.

**Band signature:** L3 (45-55) / C1-C2 / F2-F3 / R2-R3 / T1-T2 / D3-D4

**Watch-out:** This Citizen has often been dismissed by clinicians. Trust starts at T1. **Validation is the trust-building move, not protocol delivery.** Felicia's clinical signoff matters most for this variant — she IS the credibility.

**Special note:** This is one of BLEU's strongest variants. The perimenopause market is large, underserved, and willing to pay. Felicia's ACLM credential + nutrition expertise is uniquely positioned to serve this Citizen.
