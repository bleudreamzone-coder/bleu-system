# Pressure Architecture v1 — The Six Pressures

**Status:** LOCKED. Inherits from [[source_document_v1]].
**Last reviewed:** 2026-05-26

Every force below will, left unchecked, bend BLEU away from its thesis. The discipline column is the standing countermeasure. When a decision feels hard, name the pressure first — then apply its discipline. Related: [[refusal_doctrine_v1]].

---

### 1. Information pressure
The temptation to answer everything, instantly, fluently — because the model *can*.
- **Failure mode:** Confident answers to questions that deserve "I don't know" or "see a clinician." Fluency mistaken for safety.
- **Discipline:** Evidence tiers and claim boundaries on every route. Refusal is a valid, frequent output.

### 2. Nervous-system pressure
The temptation to convert by hijacking the user's fear, urgency, or shame.
- **Failure mode:** Dark patterns; selling to a dysregulated person; short-term conversion, long-term harm.
- **Discipline:** Feel → validate → solve. Crisis overrides commerce. No urgency manufacturing. (See refusals 1, 8, 10.)

### 3. Time pressure
The temptation to ship the doctrine and the demo faster than the functioning reality.
- **Failure mode:** Claims outrun the code; the deck describes a system that doesn't exist yet.
- **Discipline:** Doctrine never gets ahead of running reality (refusal 18). Scaffold → Soul-Gate → wire → smoke test.

### 4. Competitive pressure
The temptation to copy the engagement-maximizing playbook of every other app.
- **Failure mode:** Gamification, streaks, attention farming — optimizing for time-on-app over trajectory.
- **Discipline:** Trajectory is the metric, not engagement (refusal 11). Irreplaceability comes from governance, not stickiness.

### 5. Legal pressure
The temptation to either over-claim (medical authority) or freeze (claim nothing useful).
- **Failure mode:** Diagnosis-by-implication on one side; uselessly hedged non-answers on the other.
- **Discipline:** Education/wellness-support framing, clinician referral, named reviewer, audit trail. Useful *and* bounded.

### 6. Philosophical pressure
The temptation toward cosmic promises — manifestation, destiny, "the universe."
- **Failure mode:** Magical thinking dressed as wellness; certainty beyond evidence; blame when reality disagrees.
- **Discipline:** Authority is data, not theology (refusals 15, 16, 19). The variables do what they do.
