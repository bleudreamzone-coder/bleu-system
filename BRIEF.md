## I. The bleu.live Promise

bleu.live is a free platform to help you take care of yourself — without the noise, the fluff, or the upsell.

Tell us what's actually going on — sleep, stress, pain, money, grief, loneliness, anything. We'll give you simple, science-based steps you can start today.

We start with what works — not what's trending: breath, movement, food, rest, and connection — the least invasive, least expensive, most effective ways to prevent and treat most health issues.

If you need more — a supplement, a clinician, additional support — we'll point you to vetted options and tell you exactly what it costs. No gimmicks. No unnecessary products.

Create a free account so your progress saves across your devices. Pick up where you left off. Keep going.

Made with love in New Orleans — built by a clinician and the lived experience behind this platform: someone who's rebuilt, recovered, and created what they wish existed when they needed it most.

**Built on science. Shaped by real life.**

## II. Product Principles

**1. Plain language. No exceptions.**  
If a tired person at 11pm can't understand it, it doesn't ship. Clarity over cleverness. Always.

**2. One step at a time.**  
One question per screen. The user always knows where they are, what just happened, and what comes next.

**3. Behavior first. Everything else earns its place.**  
We start with what works — not what's trending: breath, movement, food, rest, connection. Supplements, services, and products come later — only if needed.

**4. Respect people's money.**  
Cost is visible. Free options come first. Lower-cost and generic alternatives are prioritized. This platform is built for real life — not unlimited budgets.

**5. Be upfront about trust.**  
We ask for an account early — and we say why: so your progress saves and you can pick up where you left off. We collect the minimum. We don't sell data. Ever.

**6. Help and selling are not the same thing.**  
Free guidance and paid options are clearly separated. No blurred lines. No hidden agendas.

**7. Make it easy to read. Or don't publish it.**  
Readable font size. Clean spacing. Strong contrast. No clutter. No visual fatigue. If it's hard to read, it's wrong.

**8. One voice. One standard.**  
No tonal whiplash. No mixing clinical authority with influencer fluff. Every interaction should feel like the same smart, grounded, trustworthy person.

**9. Science you can actually use.**  
Evidence-based, translated into action. No jargon. No overcomplication. If someone can't do something with it today, it's not finished.

**10. This is not about us.**  
We're not here to impress peers, investors, or other platforms. If it doesn't help a real person feel better today, it doesn't belong here.

**11. Integrity over everything.**  
We don't recommend what isn't needed. We don't chase trends. We don't create dependency. The goal is to help people need us less over time.

**12. Practice what we preach.**  
We use this. We live this. No burnout-built product. No advice we wouldn't follow ourselves.

**13. Progress, not perfection.**  
Small steps count. Consistency beats intensity.

## VI. Operating Doctrine

**Finish, don't add.**  
No new features, no new tabs, no new capabilities until the existing platform meets the 13 principles. The work is to fix what's drifted, not to expand the surface area.

**Monetize cleanly.**  
Sustainability is not a betrayal of the free promise. Working affiliate flows, visible paid protocols, and a referring practitioner directory keep the platform free for the people who can't pay. Revenue that meets Principles 4 and 6 (respect people's money, separate help from selling) is mission-aligned.

**Stop comparing.**  
Other platforms are not strategy inputs. Time spent benchmarking is time not spent meeting the standard. The finish line moves with every comparison and never gets crossed.

**Focus is the discipline.**  
The team's hours are finite. Every hour spent adding is an hour not spent finishing. Every hour spent comparing is an hour not spent monetizing. The platform earns its place by being focused — not by being comprehensive.
